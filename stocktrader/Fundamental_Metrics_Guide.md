## 💼 Valuation Metrics
*"How expensive is the stock?"*

### 1. P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)

**Formula**: Current Stock Price ÷ Earnings Per Share (EPS)

**What it measures**: How much investors pay for each dollar of company earnings

**Interpretation**:
- **< 15**: Undervalued (value stock territory)
- **15-25**: Fair value range for most stocks
- **> 25**: Expensive (growth premium or overvalued)

**Example**: Apple at $150 with $6 EPS = P/E of 25

**Best used for**: Comparing similar companies or vs. industry averages

**Investment insight**: Lower P/E may indicate bargain, but verify company isn't declining

---

### 2. PEG Ratio (Price/Earnings-to-Growth)

**Formula**: P/E Ratio ÷ Annual Earnings Growth Rate

**What it measures**: Whether the P/E ratio is justified by the company's growth rate

**Interpretation**:
- **< 1.0**: Attractive (growth at reasonable price - GARP investing)
- **1.0-1.5**: Reasonable valuation for growth
- **> 1.5**: Expensive relative to growth rate

**Example**: P/E of 20 with 20% earnings growth = PEG of 1.0 (fair value)

**Best used for**: Identifying growth stocks at reasonable prices

**Investment insight**: Peter Lynch's favorite metric - "fair value" stock has PEG of 1.0

---

### 3. P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)

**Formula**: Current Stock Price ÷ Book Value Per Share

**What it measures**: How much you pay for each dollar of company's net assets

**Interpretation**:
- **< 1.5**: Potentially undervalued (asset play)
- **1.5-3.0**: Reasonable for quality companies
- **> 3.0**: Premium valuation (better have strong ROE to justify)

**Best used for**: Asset-heavy businesses, banks, real estate companies

**Investment insight**: Warren Buffett often looks for P/B < 1.5 with high ROE

## 💰 Profitability Metrics
*"How profitable is the company?"*

### 4. ROE (Return on Equity) ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT

**Formula**: Net Income ÷ Shareholder Equity × 100

**What it measures**: How efficiently company uses shareholder money to generate profits

**Interpretation**:
- **> 20%**: Excellent (companies like Apple, Google, Microsoft)
- **15-20%**: Good profitability
- **10-15%**: Average performance
- **< 10%**: Weak (avoid unless special circumstances)

**Best used for**: Primary measure of management efficiency and competitive advantage

**Investment insight**: Buffett looks for consistent ROE > 15%. It's the single most important profitability metric.

---

### 5. ROA (Return on Assets)

**Formula**: Net Income ÷ Total Assets × 100

**What it measures**: How efficiently company uses ALL its assets to generate profits

**Interpretation**:
- **> 10%**: Excellent asset utilization
- **5-10%**: Good efficiency
- **< 5%**: Poor asset management

**Best used for**: Comparing companies with different capital structures

**Investment insight**: High ROA indicates efficient operations and good asset management

---

### 6. Profit Margin (Net Profit Margin)

**Formula**: Net Income ÷ Revenue × 100

**What it measures**: How much profit company keeps from each dollar of sales

**Interpretation**:
- **> 20%**: High margin business (tech, pharma, luxury brands)
- **10-20%**: Decent margins
- **5-10%**: Low margin business (retail, groceries)
- **< 5%**: Very competitive/commodity business

**Best used for**: Assessing pricing power and operational efficiency

**Investment insight**: Higher margins indicate competitive moats and pricing power

---

## 📈 Growth Metrics
*"How fast is the company growing?"*

### 7. Revenue Growth

**Formula**: (Current Year Revenue - Previous Year Revenue) ÷ Previous Year Revenue × 100

**What it measures**: How fast the company is expanding its business/market share

**Interpretation**:
- **> 15%**: High growth (exciting for growth investors)
- **5-15%**: Moderate growth (steady expansion)
- **0-5%**: Slow growth (mature company)
- **< 0%**: Declining business (value trap risk)

**Best used for**: Identifying expanding businesses vs. mature/declining ones

**Investment insight**: Sustainable revenue growth is foundation for long-term stock appreciation

---

### 8. EPS Growth (Earnings Per Share Growth) ⭐ KEY METRIC

**Formula**: (Current EPS - Previous EPS) ÷ Previous EPS × 100

**What it measures**: How fast profits per share are growing

**Interpretation**:
- **> 20%**: Strong earnings growth (growth stock)
- **10-20%**: Good growth
- **5-10%**: Moderate growth
- **< 5%**: Weak growth

**Best used for**: Primary metric for growth investors - directly drives stock price

**Investment insight**: Consistent 15%+ EPS growth often leads to significant stock appreciation

---

### 9. Earnings Growth (Total)

**Formula**: (Current Net Income - Previous Net Income) ÷ Previous Net Income × 100

**What it measures**: Total profit growth rate (absolute dollars)

**Best used for**: Understanding overall business profit expansion

**Investment insight**: Should track closely with EPS growth unless share count changes significantly

## 🏥 Financial Health Metrics
*"How financially stable is the company?"*

### 10. Debt-to-Equity Ratio ⭐ RISK INDICATOR

**Formula**: Total Debt ÷ Total Equity

**What it measures**: How much debt company uses vs. equity financing

**Interpretation**:
- **< 0.3**: Strong balance sheet (low financial risk)
- **0.3-0.6**: Moderate leverage (acceptable)
- **0.6-1.0**: High debt (risky in economic downturns)
- **> 1.0**: Very high debt (dangerous in bear markets)

**Best used for**: Assessing financial risk and recession resilience

**Investment insight**: Low debt companies survive recessions better and have more flexibility

---

### 11. Current Ratio

**Formula**: Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

**What it measures**: Company's ability to pay short-term debts with short-term assets

**Interpretation**:
- **> 2.0**: Excellent liquidity (very safe)
- **1.5-2.0**: Good liquidity
- **1.0-1.5**: Adequate but watch closely
- **< 1.0**: Liquidity problems (potential bankruptcy risk)

**Best used for**: Assessing short-term financial stability

**Investment insight**: Companies with current ratio < 1.5 may struggle in cash crunches

---

### 12. Free Cash Flow (FCF) ⭐ CRITICAL

**Formula**: Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures

**What it measures**: Cash available for dividends, buybacks, debt reduction, acquisitions

**Interpretation**:
- **Positive & Growing**: Healthy cash generation
- **Positive & Stable**: Reliable business
- **Negative**: Company spending more than generating (growth phase or trouble)

**Best used for**: Determining real cash-generating ability vs. accounting profits

**Investment insight**: Warren Buffett: "Cash is oxygen" - FCF shows real business value

## 🏛️ Market Metrics
*"How does the market perceive the stock?"*

### 13. Market Cap (Market Capitalization)

**Formula**: Current Share Price × Number of Outstanding Shares

**What it measures**: Total company value according to stock market

**Categories**:
- **> $10B**: Large Cap (stable, often dividend-paying, lower risk)
- **$2B-$10B**: Mid Cap (growth potential, moderate risk)
- **< $2B**: Small Cap (high risk/high reward potential)

**Best used for**: Determining company size category and appropriate investment allocation

**Investment insight**: Larger companies generally more stable, smaller companies more growth potential

---

### 14. Beta (Volatility Measure)

**Formula**: Statistical correlation of stock movement vs. overall market

**What it measures**: How much the stock price moves relative to market movements

**Interpretation**:
- **Beta = 1.0**: Moves exactly with market (SPY)
- **Beta > 1.0**: More volatile than market (tech stocks often 1.2-1.8)
- **Beta < 1.0**: Less volatile than market (utilities often 0.5-0.8)
- **Beta < 0**: Moves opposite to market (rare - gold miners sometimes)

**Best used for**: Portfolio risk assessment and position sizing

**Investment insight**: High beta = higher risk/reward, low beta = more stable/defensive

---

### 15. Volume (Trading Volume)

**What it measures**: Number of shares traded daily (liquidity indicator)

**Interpretation**:
- **High Volume**: Easy to buy/sell, high investor interest
- **Low Volume**: Liquidity risk, harder to exit positions

**Best used for**: Ensuring you can easily trade in/out of positions

**Investment insight**: Avoid low-volume stocks if you might need to sell quickly

---

## 💵 Income Metrics
*"Does the stock provide income?"*

### 16. Dividend Yield

**Formula**: Annual Dividends Per Share ÷ Current Stock Price × 100

**What it measures**: Annual income return from dividend payments

**Interpretation**:
- **0%**: Growth company (reinvests all profits for expansion)
- **1-3%**: Moderate dividend (growth with some income)
- **3-6%**: Good dividend yield (income focus)
- **> 6%**: Either excellent yield or dividend at risk (investigate!)

**Best used for**: Income investors seeking regular cash flow

**Investment insight**: Very high yields (>6%) often signal dividend cut risk

---

### 17. Payout Ratio

**Formula**: Dividends Per Share ÷ Earnings Per Share × 100

**What it measures**: What percentage of earnings are paid out as dividends

**Interpretation**:
- **< 40%**: Very safe dividend (lots of cushion)
- **40-60%**: Healthy dividend (sustainable)
- **60-80%**: Moderate risk (less flexibility)
- **> 80%**: High risk of dividend cuts in tough times

**Best used for**: Assessing dividend sustainability

**Investment insight**: Lower payout ratios = safer dividends + room for growth

## 🚀 Investment Decision Framework

### **The "Big 3" Metrics (Most Important)**
1. **ROE > 15%**: Indicates competitive advantage and excellent management
2. **Debt-to-Equity < 0.6**: Ensures financial stability and flexibility  
3. **Free Cash Flow**: Positive and growing shows real value creation

### **Growth vs. Value Decision Tree**

**For Growth Investing Focus On:**
- EPS Growth > 15%
- Revenue Growth > 10%
- PEG Ratio < 1.5
- ROE > 20%

**For Value Investing Focus On:**
- P/E Ratio < 15
- P/B Ratio < 1.5  
- Dividend Yield > 3%
- Strong balance sheet (low debt)

**For Income Investing Focus On:**
- Dividend Yield 3-6%
- Payout Ratio < 60%
- Consistent earnings
- Low debt-to-equity

### **Red Flags to Avoid**
- **ROE < 10%**: Poor profitability
- **Debt-to-Equity > 1.0**: Dangerous leverage
- **Current Ratio < 1.0**: Liquidity crisis risk
- **Negative Free Cash Flow**: Burning cash
- **Payout Ratio > 100%**: Unsustainable dividend

## 💡 Key Investment Insights

### **Warren Buffett's Favorites**
- ROE > 15% consistently
- Low debt-to-equity
- Predictable earnings growth
- Strong competitive moats (high profit margins)

### **Peter Lynch's Growth Approach**
- PEG ratio < 1.0 (growth at reasonable price)
- EPS growth 15-25% annually
- Reasonable P/E for growth rate
- Strong fundamentals backing growth

### **Benjamin Graham's Value Principles**
- P/E < 15
- P/B < 1.5  
- Current ratio > 2.0
- Debt-to-equity < 0.5
